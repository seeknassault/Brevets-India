LIGHTWEIGHT REHABILITATION AIDS FOR POLIO-AFFECTED CHILDREN

The main disadvantage associated with steel (metallic) rehabilitation aids is the weight that has to be borne by the user, especially so when the user is a child. A lightweight prosthesis, employing novel material, was been developed by the jointly between Prof. S. C. Lakkad of the Department of Aerospace Engineering and SDM Hospital, Jaipur. Clinical trials on over 1000 patients have confirmed the superiority and acceptability of the product in cases of lower limb paralysis.
